# Review & Sentence Playback

Review closes the loop: spaced repetition fights forgetting; **sentence playback** preserves context.

## Where Does the Queue Come From?

Words marked “unknown” during study enter the review queue. Spaced repetition schedules them roughly at:

- 1st review: ~1 day after learning
- 2nd: ~3 days
- 3rd: ~7 days
- Later: ~30 days and beyond (exact algorithm is in-app)

Successful recall lengthens the interval; failures shorten it.

## A Typical Review Session

1. Open **Today’s Review** or **Due**;
2. See (or hear) the word—**try to recall** meaning and usage first;
3. Self-check, then reveal the answer;
4. Tap **Play original sentence**: jump to the video moment from first study;
5. Rate **remembered / fuzzy / forgot** so the next interval updates.

## Why Sentence Playback Matters

Words are easier to retain in **meaningful context**. Playback restores tone, tension, and comic timing—not just a dictionary gloss.

If a word stays fuzzy, return to [Learning Session](/en/guide/learning-session) and read more surrounding lines in the same episode.

## Why This Design?

Without sentences, words become abstract symbols. Prism English keeps a one-tap **back to the line** on review so each word reconnects to the scene you watched. Background: [Why Learn This Way](/en/about/why-learn-this-way).
